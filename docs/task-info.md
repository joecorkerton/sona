# Task info

The fundamental principle of engineering at Sona is that good use of AI tooling means everyone can focus more on understanding the customer, and everyone ships product. This two part technical interview tests how well you work with coding agents, as well as what you produce

**The Brief**

You are creating a communications platform for hospitality businesses. They are trying to achieve two things:

1. Align their workforce and make them feel part of something bigger than just their day-to-day job
2. Replace WhatsApp for internal 1-to-1 and group communications

Think through what an initial POC would look like and implement a first version. You can focus on (1), (2), or go deep on either specifically.

**Submitting your take-home POC**

Share your POC with the team at least 24 hours in advance, ideally by sharing a public or private GitHub repo (we can share the interviewers' GitHub handles with you if you'd prefer to share privately).

Alongside your code, please include the artefacts that show how you worked with the AI tooling. For example your agent setup, intermediate artefacts, prompts, or standard engineering signals.

**What happens in the live session**

We'll ask you to explain how you used agents to get to your POC, and the product thinking behind it. Then we'll present various pieces of customer feedback and ask you to adapt the POC live. We'll also probe on architectural decisions and how those relate to your prompting styles and AI workflows.

**What we're assessing**

1. How you work with AI tooling to ship real product, fast. We're looking for a deliberate workflow across setup, planning, prompting, and running agents in parallel where it helps, not just that you've used a tool
2. That building a working POC like this is something you can do in under half a day
3. That you can live-iterate on customer feedback using AI tooling in real time
4. That you think about engineering from a product perspective, deeply understanding a customer problem and using code to solve it
5. That you can have product-focused conversations and directly translate them into working solutions
6. That you can articulate and defend architectural choices, and demonstrate how your AI workflow shaped them

**Stack preferences**

We're generally stack and tool agnostic. All things being equal, we prefer any combination of Elixir / TypeScript / React / React Native, but the primary focus is your ability to ship product with AI tooling, not your framework choice.
